1 Reduced Projection for CAD

This blueprint covers the formalization of Theorem 3.2.3 from McCallum’s PhD thesis An Improved Projection Operation for Cylindrical Algebraic Decomposition (1984).

Throughout, we work with \(r\)-variate polynomials viewed as univariate polynomials in a “main variable” \(x_r\) over the ring \(\mathbb {R}[x_1, \ldots , x_{r-1}]\). We set \(n = r - 1\) and write \(x = (x_1, \ldots , x_n)\).

1.1 Evaluation and specialization

Definition 1 Specialize
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • specialize

Given \(f \in \mathbb {R}[x_1,\ldots ,x_n][x_r]\) and a point \(a \in \mathbb {R}^n\), the specialization of \(f\) at \(a\) is the univariate polynomial \(f(a, x_r) \in \mathbb {R}[x_r]\) obtained by evaluating the coefficients of \(f\) (which are elements of \(\mathbb {R}[x_1,\ldots ,x_n]\)) at the point \(a\).

1.2 Order

Definition 2 Order of a polynomial at a point
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • polyOrder

The order of a polynomial \(g \in \mathbb {R}[x_1,\ldots ,x_m]\) at a point \(a \in \mathbb {R}^m\) is the smallest non-negative integer \(k\) such that the \(k\)-th iterated Fréchet derivative of the evaluation function \(x \mapsto g(x)\) at \(a\) is nonzero, or \(\infty \) if all iterated derivatives vanish.

This coincides with the smallest total degree of a nonvanishing term in the Taylor expansion of \(g\) about \(a\), which can equivalently be characterized via iterated partial derivatives (see polyOrder_eq_order’).

A function \(\theta \colon \mathbb {R}^n \to \mathbb {R}\) is a root function of \(G(x, x_r)\) on \(S\) if \(\theta (a)\) is a root of \(G(a, x_r)\) for every \(a \in S\).

Definition 11 Analytic submanifold
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • IsAnalyticSubmanifold

A nonempty subset \(S\) of \(\mathbb {R}^n\) is an analytic \(s\)-dimensional submanifold (\(0 \le s \le n\)) if for each point \(p \in S\) there is a neighborhood \(W \subseteq \mathbb {R}^n\) of \(p\) and an analytic coordinate system \(\Phi \colon W \to V\) about \(p\) such that \(S \cap W = \{ x \in W : \Phi _{s+1}(x) = 0, \ldots , \Phi _n(x) = 0 \} \). This is Definition on p. 20 of the thesis; the characterization via coordinate systems is Theorem 2.2.1.

Definition 12 Analytic delineability
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 1
  • Definition 10

Lean declarations

  • AnalyticDelineable

A polynomial \(f(x, x_r)\) is analytically delineable on a connected submanifold \(S \subseteq \mathbb {R}^n\) if there exist \(k \ge 0\) continuous functions \(\theta _1 \lt \cdots \lt \theta _k\) from \(S\) to \(\mathbb {R}\) and positive integers \(m_1, \ldots , m_k\) such that:

  1. For every \(a \in S\), \(y\) is a real root of \(f(a, x_r)\) if and only if \(y = \theta _i(a)\) for some \(1 \le i \le k\).

  2. For every \(a \in S\) and \(1 \le i \le k\), \(\theta _i(a)\) is a root of \(f(a, x_r)\) with multiplicity \(m_i\).

The graphs of the \(\theta _i\) are the \(f\)-sections over \(S\); the regions between consecutive sections are the \(f\)-sectors.

Note. The thesis requires the \(\theta _i\) to be analytic on the submanifold \(S\). Our Lean formalization currently uses ContinuousOn as a placeholder.

1.5 Squarefree basis and reduced projection

Definition 13 Squarefree basis
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • IsSquarefreeBasis

A finite set \(A\) of polynomials in \(\mathbb {R}[x_1,\ldots ,x_n][x_r]\) is a squarefree basis if:

  1. Every \(F \in A\) has positive degree in \(x_r\).

  2. Every \(F \in A\) is squarefree.

  3. The elements of \(A\) are pairwise coprime.

The reduced projection of a squarefree basis \(A\) is

Lean declarations

  • lifting_theorem

Let \(f(x, x_r)\) be a squarefree polynomial of positive degree in \(x_r\) with nonzero discriminant \(D(x)\). Let \(S\) be a connected analytic submanifold of \(\mathbb {R}^n\) in which \(f\) is degree-invariant and of non-negative degree, and in which \(D\) is order-invariant. Then \(f\) is analytically delineable on \(S\) and is order-invariant in each \(f\)-section over \(S\).

The proof (pp. 49–65) uses the Weierstrass preparation theorem, analytic coordinate changes on submanifolds, and Zariski’s 1975 theorem on equisingularity.

Lemma 21 Discriminant of product is order-invariant
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 6

Lean declarations

  • discr_prod_order_invariant

Let \(F_1, \ldots , F_m\) be squarefree pairwise coprime polynomials, and let \(S\) be a connected set. If each \(\operatorname {discr}(F_i)\) and each \(\operatorname {res}(F_i, F_j)\) (\(i \ne j\)) is order-invariant on \(S\), then \(\operatorname {discr}(\prod F_i)\) is order-invariant on \(S\).

This combines the discriminant factorization formula (Theorem 2.3.3): \(\operatorname {discr}(\prod F_i) = \prod \operatorname {discr}(F_i) \cdot \prod _{i\lt j} \operatorname {res}(F_i, F_j)^2\) with Lemma 3.2.2 (order-invariance of products).

Lemma 22 Order-invariance factors from product
✓
#

Uses

Lean declarations

  • order_invariant_full_factor_of_prod

If \(\prod F_i\) is order-invariant in a set \(T \subseteq \mathbb {R}^n \times \mathbb {R}\), then each \(F_i\) is order-invariant in \(T\).

This is the backward direction of Lemma 3.2.2 (p. 47), which states that a product of nonconstant analytic functions is order-invariant if and only if each factor is. The proof uses \(\operatorname {ord}(fg, p) = \operatorname {ord}(f,p) + \operatorname {ord}(g,p)\) and upper semicontinuity of order.

Lemma 24 Squarefree product of coprime factors
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • prod_squarefree_of_coprime

The product of squarefree pairwise coprime polynomials is squarefree.

Proof ▶

By induction on the finite set \(A\). The empty product is \(1\), which is squarefree. For the inductive step on \(\{ p\} \cup A'\) with \(p \notin A'\), we have \(\prod (\{ p\} \cup A') = p \cdot \prod A'\); it remains to verify (a) \(p\) squarefree (hypothesis), (b) \(\prod A'\) squarefree (IH), and (c) \(p\) coprime to \(\prod A'\), which follows from pairwise coprimality of \(p\) with each element of \(A'\). Mathlib’s squarefree_mul_iff concludes.

Lemma 28 Root function of factor is root function of product
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 10

Lean declarations

  • root_function_factor_of_prod

If \(\theta \) is a root function of \(G \in A\) on \(S\), then \(\theta \) is a root function of \(\prod _{F \in A} F\) on \(S\).

Proof ▶

Specialization at \(a\) and evaluation at \(\theta (a)\) are both ring homomorphisms, so they commute with the finite product: \((\prod F)(a, \theta (a)) = \prod F(a, \theta (a))\). Since \(G \in A\) and \(G(a, \theta (a)) = 0\), one factor vanishes, so the entire product vanishes.

1.7 Main result

Theorem 29 No common root of coprime polynomials
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 1

Lean declarations

  • no_common_root_of_coprime

If \(F\) and \(G\) are coprime in \(\mathbb {R}[x_1,\ldots ,x_n][x_r]\), then for every \(a \in \mathbb {R}^n\) the univariate polynomials \(F(a, x_r)\) and \(G(a, x_r)\) have no common real root.

Proof ▶

From the Bézout identity \(u F + v G = 1\) we obtain, after specialization at \(a\) and evaluation at any common root \(y\): \(0 = 1\), a contradiction.

Lean declarations

  • mccallum_3_2_3

Let \(A\) be a finite squarefree basis of \((n{+}1)\)-variate polynomials, \(r \ge 2\), and let \(S\) be a connected analytic submanifold of \(\mathbb {R}^n\). Suppose that each element of \(A\) is not identically zero on \(S\), and that each element of \(P(A)\) is order-invariant in \(S\). Then:

  1. Each element of \(A\) is degree-invariant on \(S\).

  2. Each element of \(A\) is analytically delineable on \(S\).

  3. The sections of \(A\) over \(S\) are pairwise disjoint.

  4. Each element of \(A\) is order-invariant in every section of \(A\) over \(S\).

Proof ▶

Let \(F_1, \ldots , F_m\) be the elements of \(A\) and set \(f = \prod F_i\).

(1) Degree-invariance. Each nonzero coefficient of \(F_i\) (as a polynomial in \(x_r\)) belongs to \(\operatorname {coeff}(A) \subseteq P(A)\), hence is order-invariant on \(S\). Since \(F_i\) is not identically zero on \(S\), Lemma 23 gives degree-invariance.

(3) Section disjointness. For \(F_i \ne F_j\), coprimality in \(\mathbb {R}[x][x_r]\) implies coprimality after specialization at any \(a \in S\) (the Bézout identity specializes). By Theorem 29, \(F_i(a, \cdot )\) and \(F_j(a, \cdot )\) have no common root.

(2) Delineability and (4) order-invariance in sections. Since \(f = \prod F_i\) is a product of squarefree pairwise coprime polynomials, \(f\) is squarefree (Lemma 24) with nonzero discriminant (Lemma 26). By Theorem 2.3.3, \(\operatorname {discr}(f) = \prod \operatorname {discr}(F_i) \cdot \prod _{i\lt j} \operatorname {res}(F_i, F_j)^2\). Each factor is order-invariant on \(S\) (hypothesis), so \(\operatorname {discr}(f)\) is order-invariant (Lemma 21). Also, \(f\) is degree-invariant (from (1)) and not identically zero.

By the Lifting theorem (20), \(f\) is analytically delineable on \(S\) and order-invariant in each \(f\)-section. Since the \(F_i\) are pairwise coprime, each \(F_i\)-section is an \(f\)-section, so Lemma 27 gives delineability of each \(F_i\). Lemma 22 gives that each \(F_i\) is order-invariant in every \(f\)-section (hence every section of \(A\)).
